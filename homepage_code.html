James Anthony Francese

Mathematician & Philosopher

email: math@jfrancese.com

My name is James Francese, and I'm currently a senior undergraduate studying mathematics and philosophy at Chapman University.

In mathematics, my research moves between geometry and algebra, with recent work in the deformation theory of finite-dimensional algebras and problems concerning topological groups in a topos-theoretic context. I recurringly work on areas within mathematical physics.

My philosophical work is mostly in metaphysics, epistemology, the philosophy of language, and the philosophy of mind, with a special focus on causality and ethics. I maintain strong interests in logic and the foundations of mathematics.
