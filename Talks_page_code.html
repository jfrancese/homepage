  2. Localization in Commutative Algebra: Methods for Unstable Homotopy Theory (Fullerton College, Spring 2017)

    ABSTRACT. The technique of localization with respect to a prime ideal pervades commutative algebra (the theory of modules over commutative unital rings) and algebraic geometry (the study of varieties). At the undergraduate level, many will encounter special cases of the technique in constructing the rationals from the integers and also fields of prime characteristic. We develop the necessary algebra needed to define the technique in the general setting, and show certain structures to arise naturally as local rings. Finally, we show a brief application of this technique in the area of algebraic topology known as unstable homotopy theory.
